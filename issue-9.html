THE STRAIT OF HORMUZ IS CLOSED — AND 20% OF GLOBAL OIL IS STUCK

WHAT IS HAPPENING: Iran's Revolutionary Guard fired on three container ships Wednesday and seized two of them in the Strait of Hormuz. Traffic through the strait — which handles 21 million barrels of oil per day — has effectively halted. More than 300 ships with Iranian links are now trapped on the wrong side of Trump's extended ceasefire, unable to transit without becoming targets. UK inflation data released this week already shows fuel prices rising as a direct result of the conflict.

WHY NOW: Trump extended the Iran ceasefire Tuesday, but Iran responded within 24 hours by escalating naval aggression. The timing signals Tehran believes Trump blinked first and is testing how far they can push before triggering a full military response.

WHY IT MATTERS: The Strait of Hormuz is the world's most critical energy chokepoint. A sustained closure doesn't just raise oil prices — it fractures global supply chains, forces Europe to compete for alternative energy sources, and gives China leverage as the largest buyer of Iranian crude. Treasury Secretary Bessent revealed "many" U.S. allies have requested currency swap lines to stabilise their economies amid the turbulence.

WHAT THIS MEANS: We're watching the early stages of a blockade standoff with no clear off-ramp. Iran has demonstrated it can shut down global energy flows at will. Trump's ceasefire extension bought time but not resolution — and Iran now knows the U.S. threshold for retaliation is higher than expected.

WHAT MOST PEOPLE MISS: The 300+ trapped ships aren't just a logistics problem — they're a $15B+ floating hostage situation that forces insurers, shipping companies, and commodity traders to reprice Middle East risk across every contract. The real cost isn't today's oil spike; it's the permanent risk premium that gets baked into every barrel, every shipping route, and every supply chain that touches the region for the next decade.

WHAT YOU SHOULD DO:

  • If you are a professional → Audit your company's exposure to Middle East logistics and energy inputs. Fuel surcharges and shipping delays are coming — build buffer into Q2/Q3 planning now.
  • If you are a founder → Stress-test your supply chain for a 90-day Strait closure. If you're hardware or manufacturing-dependent, lock in shipping capacity and consider pre-positioning inventory in target markets.
  • If you are an investor → Overweight energy infrastructure outside the Middle East, underweight companies with concentrated Gulf exposure. Defense and maritime security stocks are repricing upward — the move isn't done.

THE CALL: Iran just called Trump's bluff and won the first round. The ceasefire extension without consequences told Tehran everything it needed to know about U.S. resolve. This isn't de-escalation — it's a controlled escalation with Iran setting the tempo. The market hasn't priced in a six-month blockade scenario, but it should.
